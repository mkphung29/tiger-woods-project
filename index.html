The Image of Tiger Woods

Exploring Tiger Woods' career under racial and cultural lenses

Tiger Woods's impact on golf cannot be understated. After winning the 1997 Masters tournament, Woods shot up to national superstardom and became a symbol of promise for racial progress in America. His arrival on the PGA Tour generated expectations for and hope for increased diversity in professional golf, especially from non-white communities. A non-white man dominating a traditionally white, elitist sport was an incredibly powerful image for American politics and culture.

Controversies

Yet this promise of a change in America's racial politics did not happen. Tiger Woods would not be an outspoken voice against America's racial issues and disappointed many non-white people with his reluctance to make firm political stances.

Woods gives statement after cheating scandal, 2010.

Thesis

Through his assimilation into golf's white elitism and the problematic manipulation of his multiracial background, Tiger Woods upholds white prestige in American sports, culture, and politics to further his career and wealth and perpetuate an illusion of progress instead of becoming a voice for racial change in America.
